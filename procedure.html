  • Publication arrow_drop_down

    If application has Provisional Specification: Need to file Complete Specification within 12 months. If not, application will be abandoned

    If application has Complete Specification: Early or post 18 months to publish

  • Request for Examination on Form 18 arrow_drop_down

    Should be done within 48 months from date of priority

    Examination of patent is done and FER (First Examination Report) is issued

  • FER Issued arrow_drop_down

    Re-examination of amended documents

    Controller will offer hearing to Applicant

    Application should be put in order 12 months from issue of FER

    If objections met within 12 months - patent is granted and published, if not - patent will be abandoned

  • Pre-Grant Oppositionarrow_drop_down
    Once, the FER is issued, the patent applicant needs to successfully overcome the objections to receive a patent grant. The whole process may involve responding to examination reports, appearing for hearing, etc. The total time needed to put an application in order for the grant is 6 months (earlier 12 months) from the date on which the FER is issued to the applicant. However, this 6 month period can be extended for a period of 3 months by the applicant by filing a request for an extension of time (Form 4).
  • Grant of Patent arrow_drop_down
    The application would be placed in order for grant once it is found to be meeting all patentability requirements. The grant of patent is notified in the patent journal which is published time to time.
  • Post Grant Opposition arrow_drop_down
    An opposition maybe filed 12 months from the publication of a patent. The process is similar to pre-grant opposition.
  • Renewal arrow_drop_down

    After the patent has been granted, it has to be renewed every year by paying the renewal fee.

    There is no separate form required for patent renewal. A patentee can directly pay the renewal fee by making a request to the controller of patent.
